Takanashi Hoshino

The student from abydos, The vice president of the student council The president of the countermeasures committee The one said and feared to be the strongest.

Takanashi Hoshino, a koala in her school, Always sleepy, but never a fool. She smiles as if the world is light, But inside, she's tired from every fight.

She wasn't always calm and slow, Her wrath once even made anger bow. She worked the hardest, gave her all, Until her senior took the fall.

Kunichinashi Yume, the one who had led, With carefree smiles and jokes she said. But hoshino stood to guard her side, As they fought to keep their school alive

Before the great sandstorm, Abydos high was known as the strongest. But after the tradegy, everything changed, And the students were left to pay the debt.

Eventually the students left one by ome, Until only two were left under the sun. They faced the debt, the lose, the fight, Holding each other through the endless night.

But even the strongest hearts can fade, And Yume's light began to shade. No final words, no last goodbye, Just silence underneath the sky.

With long ponytail pink hair and sleep eyes, She wears her shades beneath the skies. A coat uniform without a coat. a charm so still, Yet hides strenght and iron will.

Hoshino was left with all the pain, The heavy storms, the endless rain. She laughed to hide the scars inside, And led alone, though part of her died.

Now in her third and final year, She hides her doubts and swallows fear. With tired eyes, she stands ahead, to guide her juniors in Yume's Stead

She tried to act like all was fine, A leader brave, a steady sign. But when her friend was pulled away, Her calm began to slip and sway.

she snapped beneath the weight she bore, Her warmth replaced by something more. A storm awakened in her soul, That even she could not control.

But hands reached out and pulled her near, The voices of her friends sincere. She faced her pain, stood up once more, The Hoshino her juniors swore.

She stood where storms had once begun, No longer just a girl who'd run. With wounds still fresh, she chose to lead, A shield for those who still believe.
