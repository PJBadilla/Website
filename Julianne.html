Buntod Reef: The Place You'll Love To Be

Beneath The water so crystal clear,
The reef below felt near.
The perfect warm breeze,
Always makes my heart at peace.

Everyone laugh along the shore,
In buntod's charm, we ask no more.
May hearts unite to guard this land,
This beatiful gem made by nature's hand

Here, times slows down, the souls find rest-
In buntod's heart, we know we're blessed.
Buntod lives in our hears that cam
and calls the ocean-lovers home.

Admiring From A Far: Liking You Was the Best Choice

Half-asleep and still fine-how is that fair,
He's cheeks when pinched soft as teddy bear.
Assignments done in half the time, barely even care
Similarities attract, i'm aware.

Maybe one day we'll laught and snack, just two wings in hand,
Because loving fried chicken together? Now that sounds grand.
Now I'm here downbad on someone.
Crushing hard on a perfect sporty man.

Some say it's just a phase im in,
I mean liked by a matured boy like him? it is for a sure a win.
Tall and handsome, like a dream i never thought I'd chase,
His sweet vanilla scrent perfume I'd like to embrace.

He steps onto the court with grace, a shuttle in the air.
A sport, genious boy, both fierce and fair.
For some reasons, I recommend to be unseen,
Secrets kept, where hearts stay serene.

Title: The Missing Dog Caper
Author: Billy Dixon
Genre: Mystery
Pages: 88

Part 2: Summary
The story follows Patty Parker, a young girl delivering newspapers during summer break to earn money for a bicycle and a gift for her grandmother. Title: Chrissa Stands Strong
Author: Mary Cassanova
Genre: Realistic Fiction, Children’s Literature
Pages: 151

Part 2: Summary
“Chrissa Stands Strong” is the story of Chrissa Maxwell, a young girl starting fourth grade who faces bullying and social challenges at school. Set in an American town, the story follows Chrissa as she navigates the complexities of friendship and standing up for what’s right. The central conflict arises when Chrissa and her friends become the target of bullying by a group of classmates, leading to feelings of isolation and helplessness. The story resolves as Chrissa finds the courage to speak out against bullying with the help of her parents and teacher.

Part 3: Main Character
Chrissa Maxwell – A kind and compassionate fourth-grader who struggles with self-doubt and bullying but eventually finds her voice.

Part 4: Reflection
This book teaches the importance of standing up against bullying and finding the courage to speak out even when it is difficult. It also highlights the value of friendship and support.

Unlocking literary worlds: A journey through reading and writing

This quarter 1, the grade 7 students of Masbate National Comprehensive High School (MNCHS) embarked on an intensive literary arts lessons, focusing on evaluating texts and composing culturally reflective works.

Through diverse lessons on poetic forms, character analysis, and literary devices, we dive deep into the heart of literature, exploring poetry's intricate elements and diverse types, from Tanaga to free verse, we identify characters, conflicts, and the rich tapestry of figures of speech, using these insights to compose our own original works reflecting Filipino culture and identity, by understanding maxims, universal truths, and socio-cultural contexts, we foster empathy, revise for cohesion, and ultimately publish poems that speak volumes.

We actively created various poems weekly, applying theoretical knowledge, and conducted seven weekly home reading reports as performance tasks, fostering critical engagement with literature.

This comprehensive approach allowed us to both understand and create compelling literary pieces, culminating in our own published works.
