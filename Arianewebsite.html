Buntod Reef: The Place You'll Love To Be

A destination, calm and bright,
Sunrise comes up, a glorious light.
Clear skies above, a sunlit scene,
Buntod's beachespristine and keen.

Relaxing in the sun, feeling the warmth so deep,
laying there as I tan, letting my spirit sleep.
The clear blue sea, a peaceful rest,
Buntod's embrace, truly blessed.

Attractions draw, a curious eye,
Nature's wonders, reaching high.
Relaxing waves, a gentle sound,
Island beauty, all around.

The sun dips low, a fiery kiss,
On Buntod's shores, a soothing bliss.
The waves they sigh, a gentle sound,
As peace descends, on hallowed ground.

Sunset hues, a fiery grace,
Province's charm, a peaceful space.
So come and feel the ocean's might,
In Buntod's charm, bathed in soft light.

The Silent Blade

The walks alone where shadows creep.
A watchful soul, his silence deep.
No boastful words, no need to prove,
In quiet steps, he makes his move.

A heart restrained behind cold eyes,
But in that stillness, kindness lies.
He bears the guilt of battles past,
Yet fights with honor, firm and fast.
He shields the weak, though few may see
The quiet depth of loyalty.

A steady storm, a guiding flame,
He never fights for praise or fame.
With duty bound and sorrow worn,
He stands alone, both scarred and sworn.
Yet even when he hides the pain,
His soul flows strong - like gentle rain.

Underneath the Blossom Tree: A Wish for Joyful Laughs

Underneath the blossom tree,
there we once met with promises to keep.
We were sweat hearts,
Love and care, unaware of the stories you dare to care.

As the years go by,
You've became distant and secretive.
As I supported you financially and physically.
I struggled but I didn't get in your way.

I was blind by our love that I taught was there,
All you gave now is coldness stares.
Wish we go back to what were before,
With joyful laughs, and love one another.

The Poet's Unwritten Verse

I longed to be the poem, flowing, free,
A whispered verse for all the world to see,
A song of starlight, wild as the sea,
But fate, it seems, had other plans for me.

I yearned to be the rhythm, soft and deep,
A lullaby that lulls the world to sleep,
A secret that the silent mountains keep,
But in my hands, the pen began to creep.

I dreamt of being words on pages bright,
A beacon burning in the darkest night,
A guiding star, a pure and gentle light,
But found myself still writing, day and night.

I tried to lose myself in ink and quill,
To let the verses flow against my will,
To capture beauty, standing deathly still,
But every line revealed my poet's skill.

So here I stand, the poet, not the art,
With ink stained fingers and a heavy heart,
Forever bound to play the destined part,
And write the poems that tear my soul apart.

Unlocking literary worlds: A journey through reading and writing

This quarter 1, the grade 7 students of Masbate National Comprehensive High School (MNCHS) embarked on an intensive literary arts lessons, focusing on evaluating texts and composing culturally reflective works.

Through diverse lessons on poetic forms, character analysis, and literary devices, we dive deep into the heart of literature, exploring poetry's intricate elements and diverse types, from Tanaga to free verse, we identify characters, conflicts, and the rich tapestry of figures of speech, using these insights to compose our own original works reflecting Filipino culture and identity, by understanding maxims, universal truths, and socio-cultural contexts, we foster empathy, revise for cohesion, and ultimately publish poems that speak volumes.

We actively created various poems weekly, applying theoretical knowledge, and conducted seven weekly home reading reports as performance tasks, fostering critical engagement with literature.

This comprehensive approach allowed us to both understand and create compelling literary pieces, culminating in our own published works.
